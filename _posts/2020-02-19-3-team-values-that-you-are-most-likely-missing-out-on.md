When it comes to team values, people tend to think quite lightly of them and sometimes even disregard them because in the end they are just a bunch of fancy hyped words. Is that really true? No… Most teams have real values and work hard to implement these values into their every day development work. However, some values can be better then others, so before we look at some examples, let’s first define what it means to have team values.

Obviously, if you are just having values for the sake of having values, it will not get you anywhere. Nonetheless, even if you have established your core values, how do these values actually integrate into your development process?

The values should be of such kind that they will help you as a team to achieve your long term goals and wishes. Values also mean that you would be willing to sacrifice something else in the short term in order to better achieve your goals. Maybe you value quality over efficiency and therefore consume more capacity for testing features because you believe that in the end it will result into less bugs, although perhaps ultimately forcing you to complete fewer features.

Besides aiding the team goals and wishes, values also contribute to certain behaviors and attitudes within the team. This is quite self explanatory, certain values endorse team members to for example exert team work or show co-responsibility. But did you realize that this also goes for negative behaviors or attitudes within the team? For example, if you notice that individuals within the team react negatively towards those who have difficulties understanding something, then perhaps one of their values might be knowledge. Obviously this is something that should be address within the team and with those specific individuals but this brings me to the next point. Why do we want to establish team values?

When you establish your team values it is actually a very eye opening experience because you learn a lot about how your team members view the world around them. When learning about each others values and reaching your common team values, you create a stronger bond and discover more about each others personalities. However, not every human being is able to happily co-exist with everyone and this is why these common values are so important. You essentially together agree on common types of behaviors and attitudes within the team so that all team members can work and co-exist more fluently while working towards common goals. Also, only when you have identified the team values, you are able to talk about them on a deeper level and create actions towards fulfilling the established values.

Now, that we have talked about having team values, let’s have a look at some values and concretely analyze how they might influence the team and how one value can actually cover multiple values through indirect consequences of the core value itself.

- **Quality Built-in**  
  With quality built-in, you ensure that with each step you take within your development process, you receive feedback on your progress. Using this feedback, you can verify whether you achieved what you aimed for. This creates confidence and certainty in your deliverables. When you as a team trust the performance and quality of your deliverables, you are also inspired to innovate and developing becomes a whole lot more fun!

- **Transparency**  
  When decisions, workflows and processes are transparent, you have the ability to swiftly react to always changing circumstances. Not only that, through transparency you advocate a common understanding allowing for much more fluent communication and cooperation. With transparency in all work that the team does, it becomes much easier to initiate knowledge sharing and in some cases also identify bottlenecks in the development process.

- **Trust**  
  You trust others with what they promise and you trust yourself with what you promise. With trust in others and yourself, you also build a certain level of respect and encourage responsibility and accountability. Although difficult at times, acknowledging who is responsible and accountable for what, creates the opportunity to also clearly communicate about too much or too little responsibility. In ideal situations, this should also result into a feeling of shared responsibility, which in turn sparks even more communication. We all know that communication is key to successful and efficient cooperation.

The key point to take away from all of this is that it is possible to create values which indirectly also promote other important values. For example, looking at quality built-in, it indirectly also promotes work satisfaction and allows for more enjoyment at the work place. Many teams value having fun while working and it is possible to set this as a value directly but the value itself does not indicate anything about what the team perceives as a fun work place and how it is achieved. Therefore, in short, make sure not to mix up your team wishes as values with the true values of your team that contribute to your team goals and wishes.
